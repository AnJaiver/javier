Production Analysis Bias

In the production level analysis, we mainly take a political economy perspective and want to talk about how advanced AI has chained us in filter bubbles and widened knowledge gap of our society

Create Echo Chambers

Personalized Content Recommendations
Advanced AI algorithms often personalize content recommendations based on users' preferences and past behavior. While this aims to enhance user experience, it can inadvertently create filter bubbles. Users are more likely to be exposed to information and opinions that align with their existing beliefs, preferences, and online activities. This leads to a self-reinforcing cycle where individuals are increasingly isolated from alternative viewpoints, narrowing their understanding of complex issues.
Manipulation of Public Discourse
Advanced AI systems can generate highly persuasive and realistic content, including fake news, propaganda, and politically motivated narratives. This capability allows malicious actors to exploit AI for the purpose of manipulating public opinion. By disseminating tailored messages that reinforce existing biases, these actors can reinforce echo chambers and foster divisions within society, making it more challenging for different groups to find common ground or engage in constructive dialogue.

Widen Knowledge Gap

Unequal Access to Advanced AI
Developing and deploying advanced AI systems requires significant computational resources, technical expertise, and access to large datasets. As a result, these technologies are more accessible to individuals, organizations, or countries with the necessary resources and capabilities. This creates a digital divide, where those who lack access to advanced AI systems are at a disadvantage in terms of accessing and benefiting from the information they provide. This unequal access widens the knowledge gap between different segments of society.
Reinforcement of Existing Inequalities
Advanced AI systems, when deployed in sectors such as education, can perpetuate existing educational inequalities. If access to AI-driven educational tools and resources is limited to certain groups or institutions, those who lack access may fall further behind in acquiring knowledge and skills. This reinforces disparities in educational opportunities and exacerbates the knowledge gap between different socioeconomic groups.
